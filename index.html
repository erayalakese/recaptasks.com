Common questions

Why is Slack integration free? +

Because Slack, Teams, and Zapier are how you get value from Recap — not how we make money. Competitors like Geekbot charge $2.50/user/month for Slack alone. Recap gives all three integrations to every user, free forever. The only paid feature is unlimited history.

What happens when my 14-day history limit is reached? +

On day 12, Recap warns you that your oldest entry will roll off in 2 days. On day 15, the oldest day is removed to make room for the new one. Pro users keep every day, forever — no expiry, no rolling window.

How is Recap different from Todoist or Notion? +

Todoist and Notion are full project management systems — they require setup, structure, and ongoing maintenance. Recap is a daily-only tool. No projects, no labels, no hierarchy. Open it, type today's tasks, and close it. The scope is intentionally narrow.

What happens to tasks I don't finish? +

They roll over to tomorrow's list automatically at midnight. You'll see them marked as carried over at the top of your list the next morning. Nothing disappears.

Is there a mobile app? +

The web app is fully mobile-optimized. A native mobile app is on the roadmap.

What happens to my data if I downgrade from Pro? +

Your full history stays intact. The rolling 14-day window takes effect again, but nothing is deleted immediately. Upgrade again and it picks up exactly where you left off.

Is Recap a free Notepad alternative for task tracking? +

Yes. Recap is a free Notepad alternative built for daily task tracking. Like Notepad, you open it and start typing immediately with no setup. Unlike Notepad, tasks get checkboxes, incomplete tasks roll over at midnight automatically, and you get a searchable day-by-day history with a one-click standup summary.
